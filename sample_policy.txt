# ACME Corporation Cybersecurity Policy

## 1. Purpose and Scope
This policy establishes guidelines for protecting ACME Corporation's information assets and technology infrastructure. It applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties with access to company systems.

## 2. Password Policy
2.1 All passwords must be at least 12 characters long
2.2 Passwords must contain a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters
2.3 Passwords must be changed every 90 days
2.4 Previous 8 passwords cannot be reused
2.5 Multi-factor authentication is required for all administrative accounts

## 3. Data Protection
3.1 All sensitive data must be encrypted both in transit and at rest
3.2 Customer personally identifiable information (PII) must be stored only in approved systems
3.3 Data must be classified according to sensitivity levels: Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted
3.4 Regular data backups must be performed and tested quarterly

## 4. Access Control
4.1 Access to systems and data must follow the principle of least privilege
4.2 All access must be reviewed quarterly by department managers
4.3 User accounts must be disabled immediately upon employee termination
4.4 Privileged access requires management approval and additional monitoring

## 5. Incident Response
5.1 All security incidents must be reported to the Security Operations Center within 1 hour
5.2 A designated Incident Response Team will coordinate all response activities
5.3 Regular incident response drills must be conducted semi-annually
5.4 Post-incident reviews must be documented and used to improve security controls

## 6. Compliance
6.1 Annual security awareness training is mandatory for all staff
6.2 Quarterly compliance assessments will be conducted by the Security team
6.3 Third-party security assessments must be performed annually
6.4 Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action
